Electronics
& control

An isolated 220 V power cascade, PID-regulated heating, and an Arduino finite-state machine that turns a potentiometer and two buttons into a deterministic casting cycle.

01 — Electrical architecture

Two worlds, kept apart.

The system runs two electrically separate domains: a 220 V AC side feeding the mica heater through a solid-state relay, and a low-voltage DC side (an ATX supply delivering +12 V and +5 V) powering the logic, the stepper driver, and the fans.

Mains enters through an IEC inlet with an integrated EMI filter, passes a 16 A circuit breaker and mechanical KCD phase-breaking switches, and only then reaches the heater loop. This protective cascade keeps the operator isolated from the AC path and prevents electrical noise from corrupting the control signals.

Heat control

Bed temperature is closed-loop regulated by an Inkbird ITC-100VH PID controller reading a K-type thermocouple and switching the 800 W mica heater through a solid-state relay. The thermocouple sits at the plate edge, which reads slightly cooler than the centre; the offset is corrected in the PID configuration so the coating surface holds its true setpoint.

02 — Firmware

A four-state machine.

The casting cycle is modelled as a finite-state machine — the structure that finally eliminated the motor stutter of earlier versions and made every run deterministic and repeatable.

Idle — waiting for start

The motor is disabled and silent. The LCD shows the live speed the operator dials in on the potentiometer. Nothing moves until the start button is pressed with a non-zero speed.

Coating — forward stroke

On start, the chosen velocity is locked — frozen from the potentiometer so it can’t drift mid-cast. The carriage drives forward at that constant speed until it trips the end limit switch. The LCD is intentionally not refreshed here, to avoid injecting stutter into the motor timing.

End hold

Casting complete. The motor is released and the display prompts for a reset — a clean, unambiguous stopping point.

Return — home

On reset, the carriage reverses at a fixed 45 mm/s back to the home limit switch, then loops to Idle, ready for the next cast.

03 — Speed control

Locked at the trigger.

Traverse speed is read from a potentiometer over a 0–45 mm/s range and converted to a step delay by a simple system constant: step delay (µs) = 3333 / velocity at 1600 microsteps. A hysteresis filter ignores changes smaller than a few ADC counts, so electrical noise can’t make the speed jitter.

Crucially, the value is latched at the start-button press and held for the entire forward stroke. This is the software half of the constant-shear guarantee: the operator physically cannot nudge the speed mid-cast.

Safety-critical finding

The drying analysis carried a warning into the electronics: without active ventilation, the sealed chamber would exceed the lower explosive limit for ethanol vapour in about 90 seconds. That reclassified the lid fans from a comfort feature to a safety-critical extraction system — one of the project’s defining systems-engineering lessons, since an isolated subsystem view had first treated them as merely thermal.
