Manufacturing
and cost

Where CAD meets the workshop. Custom aluminium machining, EDM micro-drilling, and a build economy stretched by institutional and industry support.

01 — Materials & processes

Built from accessible stock, machined with precision.

M-01

Bimetallic chuck

Al 5083 base (20 mm) and Al 2045 top plate (5 mm) — chosen for closely matched thermal expansion. CNC lathe and milling cut the vacuum channels and the O-ring seal groove.

M-02

EDM micro-holes

The 117 vacuum holes couldn’t be drilled conventionally — thin bits break and walk off. EDM fast-hole drilling erodes clean, precise holes with no tool deflection.

M-03

Frame and enclosure

An MDF base with PTFE feet isolates the heated assembly and damps vibration; sheet-metal enclosure and bent panels house the drive and electronics bay.

02 — The manufacturing challenge

Why the first plan failed.

The original intent was to drill the top-plate holes on a drill press and CNC mill. It didn’t survive contact with reality: the thin bits snapped, chip evacuation was poor, and the tool wandered off position on such small diameters.

The corrective measure was EDM fast-hole drilling — a spark-erosion process with high-pressure flushing that ignores tool-deflection limits entirely, producing repeatable micro-holes. It’s a textbook example of letting the manufacturing constraint, not the ideal CAD geometry, drive the process choice.

03 — Assembly

Bolted, not welded.

The machine is deliberately an open, modular assembly — standard fasteners rather than permanent welds — so heaters, motors, and the blade can be swapped, upgraded, or cleaned independently. Linear rails bolt to the frame; the enclosure and lid attach with hinges and gas struts for maintenance access.

Integration surfaced the real-world lessons: micro-stepping vibration killed solderless breadboard connections, and the bimetallic chuck demanded tight machining tolerances. Both pointed to the same conclusion — industrial-grade assembly methods matter more than an ideal drawing suggests.
