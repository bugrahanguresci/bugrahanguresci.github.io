A two-person, multidisciplinary capstone — mechanical design, mechatronics, control, and process modelling under one roof.

01 — Who did what

Divided by domain, integrated as a system.

Both members shared the conceptualization, literature review, general assembly, and final report. Primary responsibilities split along technical lines:

ROLE A

Buğrahan Güreşçi

MECHATRONICS · CONTROLS · THERMODYNAMICS

Electrical architecture and the 220 V protective cascade; the Arduino finite-state-machine firmware; and the thermodynamic analyses — drying kinetics, convective mass transfer of the extraction fans, and the fluid-dynamics modelling behind the blade-gap window.

ROLE B

Muhammet Ali Doğan

MECHANICAL DESIGN · MANUFACTURING

CAD modelling of the subsystems and the physical workshop fabrication of the custom aluminium and stainless components — CNC milling the vacuum chuck and machining the doctor blade among them.

Integration

Final system integration, troubleshooting, and the ME 492 report were executed collaboratively. The project demanded balancing thermodynamics, fluid kinematics, and mechatronic safety simultaneously — a systems-engineering exercise where optimising one subsystem in isolation could compromise the whole machine.

About this site

This documentation site — its content, structure, and front-end implementation — was designed and built by Buğrahan Güreşçi.

02 — Project management

Three milestones, all realised.

Design freeze

All theoretical calculations, 3D CAD models, and the Bill of Materials finalised — first month of the spring term.

Subsystem fabrication complete

Bimetallic chuck, U-frame, and enclosure machined and verified against dimensional tolerances — mid-term.

Full system integration

Mechanical, electrical, and pneumatic systems assembled into a single unit executing a stutter-free casting cycle — late term.
